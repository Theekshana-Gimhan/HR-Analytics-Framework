
# **User Personas: Simpala HR**

This document details the primary user personas for the Simpala HR platform, based on the target audience of Small and Medium Businesses (SMBs) in Sri Lanka.

---

### **Persona 1: Nimal Perera, The Business Owner**

*   **Role:** Owner of a 30-employee light manufacturing business.
*   **Background:** Nimal is a hands-on entrepreneur, deeply involved in all aspects of his business, from operations to finance. He is not an HR expert and wears multiple hats. His primary focus is on business growth and profitability.
*   **Goals:**
    *   Ensure his business is 100% compliant with Sri Lankan labor laws (EPF, ETF, PAYE) to avoid legal issues and fines.
    *   Reduce the time he and his staff spend on manual administrative tasks.
    *   Maintain tight control over operational costs, including payroll.
    *   Get a quick, clear overview of his workforce, such as daily attendance, leave schedules, and total monthly salary expenses.
*   **Frustrations:**
    *   Wasting valuable time on paperwork that could be spent on growing the business.
    *   Constant worry about making costly calculation errors in EPF/ETF payments.
    *   Lack of a single, reliable source for employee information; data is scattered across spreadsheets, physical files, and his own memory.
    *   Approving leave is informal and hard to track, leading to scheduling conflicts.

### **Persona 2: Anusha Silva, The Admin Manager**

*   **Role:** Admin & Accounts Manager for a 50-person digital marketing agency. She is the designated "power user" of Simpala HR.
*   **Background:** Anusha is responsible for a wide range of administrative and financial duties. She is detail-oriented and tasked with the critical responsibility of ensuring everyone gets paid correctly and on time. She is comfortable with technology but frustrated by inefficient processes.
*   **Goals:**
    *   Run the monthly payroll process accurately and efficiently, with minimal manual calculations.
    *   Manage all employee leave requests in a streamlined, digital manner.
    *   Maintain pristine, up-to-date, and easily accessible employee records.
    *   Generate all necessary statutory reports (for EPF, ETF, PAYE) without the usual hassle and potential for errors.
*   **Frustrations:**
    *   The tedious and error-prone nature of manual data entry across multiple, often inconsistent, Excel spreadsheets.
    *   Constantly having to chase employees for paper-based leave application forms.
    *   The complex and stressful process of calculating payroll, which involves various allowances, deductions, and no-pay adjustments.
    *   The lack of a centralized system makes it difficult to answer simple employee queries, like their remaining leave balance.
