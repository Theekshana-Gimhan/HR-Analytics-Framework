# Reference [8]: Cost of Employee Turnover (D. G. Allen, 2008)

### **The "Rule of Thumb" Figures**
Turnover is more expensive than most managers realize.
*   **General Replacement Cost:** **90% to 200%** of an employee's annual salary.
*   **Mid-Level/Professional:** 150% to 200%.
*   **Executive:** Up to 400%.

### **Cost Categories**
1.  **Separation:** Exit interviews and admin.
2.  **Vacancy:** Overtime for others and **Lost Productivity**.
3.  **Replacement:** Advertising, screening, and interviewing.
4.  **Training:** Orientation and the "ramp-up" period for the new hire.

### **Use in Viva**
"According to D.G. Allen (2008), losing one employee can cost an SME up to 200% of their annual salary. If my system identifies even **one** high-risk employee who is then retained, it has already paid for its entire LKR 10,000/month cost for several years."
