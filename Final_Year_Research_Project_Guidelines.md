# Final Year Individual Research Project Guidelines (COM4901)

**Module Title:** Final Year Individual Project  
**Module Code:** COM 4901  
**Faculty:** Faculty of Computer Science and Engineering, KIU University  

---

## 1. Introduction
The Final Year Individual Research Project (COM4901) is a compulsory capstone module. It provides students with the opportunity to demonstrate independent research capability, professional responsibility, and technical competence by planning, developing, implementing, and evaluating an original project in an appropriate computing domain.

## 2. Learning Outcomes of the Module
By the end of this module, students should be able to:
* **LO1:** Develop a project proposal to solve a problem in a relevant area/domain.
* **LO2:** Identify ethical or professional issues relevant to the project topic.
* **LO3:** Carry out a literature survey related to the topic.
* **LO4:** Develop a methodology to design and implement a solution.
* **LO5:** Implement, test, evaluate, and validate the solution/prototype.
* **LO6:** Demonstrate oral and written communication skills in prototypes, demos, and documentation.

## 3. Types of Projects
Students may undertake either a Research-Oriented or a Development-Oriented project.

### 3.1 Research-Oriented Project
Involves investigating a research problem or gap through a structured methodology (e.g., ML model comparison, cyber security evaluation frameworks).

### 3.2 Development-Oriented Project
Involves designing and implementing a computing solution to address a real-world problem (e.g., web/mobile applications, IoT systems, automation solutions).

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

### 4.1 Student Responsibilities
* Select an appropriate topic and obtain an approved supervisor.
* Maintain a **Project Diary/Logbook**.
* Follow ethical practices and ensure academic integrity.
* Submit reports and prepare for the final presentation/viva.
* Ensure proper backup and version control of project files.

### 4.2 Supervisor Responsibilities
* Guide the student in topic refinement and problem statement.
* Provide academic guidance and monitor progress.
* Conduct regular supervision meetings (minimum once every two weeks).
* Identify risks and report serious issues.

## 5. Project Process and Timeline
Standard milestones include:
1. Topic Selection & Supervisor Confirmation
2. Proposal Submission
3. Proposal Presentation
4. Project Execution & Monitoring
5. Interim Progress Review
6. Final Thesis Submission
7. Final Presentation & Viva

### 5.1 Assessment Components and Weightage
| Assessment Component | Weightage (%) |
| :--- | :--- |
| Proposal Writing | 15 |
| Presenting the Proposal | 10 |
| Interim Report | 15 |
| Submission of Final Report | 30 |
| Presentation and Demonstration | 30 |
| **Total** | **100** |

## 6. Proposal Guidelines
* **Format:** Font: Times New Roman, Size: 12, Spacing: 1.5, Margins: 1 inch.
* **Page Limit:** Maximum 10 pages.
* **Structure:** Title Page, Introduction, Problem Statement, Objectives, Scope, Literature Summary, Methodology, Tools/Resources, Plan/Timeline, References (IEEE).

## 7. Progress Monitoring & Diary Requirement
Students **must maintain a Project Diary / Logbook** as evidence of progress, including meeting dates, supervisor feedback, tasks completed, and challenges faced.

## 8. Ethical Considerations
Projects involving human participants, personal data, biometric data, or camera-based data collection require **ethical approval**.

## 9. Interim Progress Review and Interim Report
* **Contribution:** 15% of the final grade.
* **Length:** 10–20 pages.
* **Content:** Progress summary, literature review status, methodology, design/implementation progress, and updated work plan.

## 10. Dissertation / Thesis Guidelines
### 10.2 Formatting Requirements
* Font: Times New Roman, Size: 12, Spacing: 1.5, Margins: 1 inch.
* Referencing Style: IEEE referencing.

### 10.3 Standard Dissertation Structure
1. Title Page
2. Abstract
3. Acknowledgement
4. Table of Contents
5. List of Figures / Tables
6. Chapter 1 – Introduction
7. Chapter 2 – Literature Review
8. Chapter 3 – Methodology / Design
9. Chapter 4 – Implementation
10. Chapter 5 – Testing & Evaluation
11. Chapter 6 – Conclusions and Future Work
12. References & Appendices

## 11. Academic Integrity & Plagiarism Policy
Plagiarism is strictly prohibited. All documents must be submitted through plagiarism checking tools (e.g., Turnitin).

## 12. Presentation and Viva (Dissertation Defence)
Assesses technical understanding, justification of design decisions, and demonstration of results. Students must bring presentation slides, system demo, and the dissertation document.

## 13. Final Submission Requirements
Must include the Final Dissertation (PDF and Word), presentation slides, source code/executable, datasets (if applicable), plagiarism report, and project diary/logbook.
